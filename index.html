§ 02 — Approach

Three promises. Held at the sensor.

P.01

Trust-first,
from the device up.

Every observation is anchored to a specific device, place, and moment — and signed before it leaves the field. What you receive is not a data point. It is testimony.

P.02

Built to wait,
by design.

The field node architecture is built around duty-cycled wake, solar + LiFePO4 power, and a secure element that never streams raw data — a deliberate hardware design, now moving from architecture to build. See the roadmap.

P.03

Edge intelligence,
no cloud tether.

Events are interpreted locally — waking compute only when something meaningful happens. Less energy, less bandwidth, less dependence on anyone else’s uplink.

§ 03 — Method

Four movements.
One sealed record.

TerraProof does not stream raw data. It watches, interprets, seals — and hands you a record that travels without losing its integrity.

01 · DETECT

Watch for what matters.
Ignore what doesn’t.

A YOLOv26n detection pass scans every frame and flags what's worth attending to — every animal in shot, not just one. Built to eventually run behind a duty-cycled wake trigger in the field; today it runs against pilot and public camera-trap imagery.

02 · INTERPRET

On-device intelligence.
No cloud tether.

When an event occurs, TerraProof wakes local compute to classify it — an EfficientNet V2-M classifier scoring against a 2,498-species taxonomy. Species, individual count, confidence — resolved per frame, no cloud round-trip required.

03 · SIGN

Tamper-evident,
sealed in place.

The event is bound to its device, coordinates, and timestamp, hashed, and signed with the private key before it ever touches a network. Alter one field afterward and the signature breaks — visibly, specifically, provably. Try it below.

Evidence that travels
without losing weight.

§ 07 — Field notes

Questions we get,
answered plainly.

Q.01 What is TerraProof, in one sentence?
+
A working edge AI pipeline that detects and classifies wildlife, cryptographically signs every observation, and scores ecosystem condition — with a physical field node as the next step.
Q.02 Is this just a smarter camera trap?
+
No. Camera traps record. TerraProof interprets, signs, and vouches. The output is a structured, tamper-evident event — not a media file you have to defend after the fact.
Q.03 How does it differ from ordinary monitoring tools?
+
Most tools optimise for volume of data. TerraProof optimises for integrity per event — structure, traceability, and cryptographic proof baked in at the sensor.
Q.04 Who is it for?
+
Conservation projects, restoration operators, biodiversity monitoring teams, researchers, and corporate sustainability teams who need their field evidence to survive scrutiny from regulators, auditors, or registries.
Q.05 Is the hardware available yet?
+
Not yet — the physical field node is Phase D on our roadmap (see above), and we're building toward it deliberately rather than promising a ship date before it's earned. What is available today is the software: the detection and scoring pipeline, and the full dashboard, which you can request a walkthrough of now.
Q.06 Does this replace eDNA, acoustics, or satellite?
+
No — and in fact we fuse with one of them directly: real Sentinel-2 satellite NDVI (vegetation greenness) feeds our own Structure sub-index alongside the fauna signal from the sensor. Think of TerraProof as the trust layer and fusion engine sitting across these methods, not a replacement for any one of them. (NDVI is a greenness proxy, not biodiversity itself — we treat it as one signal, never the whole picture.)
Q.07 Is the Ecosystem Condition Index certified or independently validated?
+
Not yet, and we say so on every score. The reference conditions and index weights are provisional, self-baseline estimates pending review by a qualified ecologist. Every score ships with a confidence tier and its full component breakdown — never a bare number. Cryptographic proof tells you the data wasn't altered; it doesn't by itself tell you the ecology is sound, and we never blur that line.
